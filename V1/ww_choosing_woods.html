This is a high overview of some of the commonly used woods out there...

Most of the time, wood is chosen for its color and hardness. The description below will not be true for every piece of that particular wood as it varies from tree to tree, although the information give is what they will most likely resemble.

Alder
Color: tan
Hardness: soft
Comments: Similar to pine. It is can be used to make furniture, but not a good outdoor wood.
Balsa
Color: light tan
Hardness: extremely soft
Comments: One of the softest woods in the world. Because of this, it can be used for wooden models.
Bamboo
Color: yellow tan
Hardness: hard
Comments: because of its tight grains, bamboo is strong even when cut in small strips. It can be used to make furniture, small boxes, and pens.
Birch
Color: tan
Hardness: medium hard
Comments: Similar to alder in color but is has greater hardness. It is used to make furniture and small boxes.
Cedar
Color: red brown
Hardness: soft
Comments: Has rot resistant properties that make it great for outdoor use. Can be used to build furniture for indoor or out.
Cherry
Color: tan brown
Hardness: Hard
Comments: Used to make high end furniture, instruments, pen, and cutting boards.
Douglas Fir
Color: reddish-tan
Hardness: medium-soft
Comments: One of the least expensive woods on the market. Used in house construction.
Hemlock
Color: tan
Hardness: soft
Comments: Similar to Douglas Fir in both price, hardness, and use.
Mahogany
Color: red
Hardness: hard
Comments: Used to make high end furniture, cutting boards, pens, boxes, instruments.
Maple
Color: tan white
Hardness: medium hard
Comments: Used to make furniture, baby toys, cutting boards, and boxes.
Oak
Color: tan
Hardness: hard
Comments: One of the least expensive of the hardwoods and can be used to make most anything.
Pine
Color: tan yellow
Hardness: soft
Comments: Rots easily and is used to make some furniture and sheaving.
Purple Heart
Color: purple
Hardness: hard
Comments: because of its high cost, it is generally use to make pens, cutting board, and small boxes.
Marble Wood
Color: yellow tan with brown streaks
Hardness: extremely hard
Comments: Used in high end furniture and making pens.
