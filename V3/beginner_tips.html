Sand Everything Smooth

Varnish and paint don't cover up rough surfaces. It actually makes them more apparent. Sanding surfaces smooth is one of the most important elements to making professional woodwork.

Laminate Woods with Alike Hardness

Woods with different hardness require different levels of sandpaper grit to be smoothed out. When you come sand your wooden creation, the softer wood will remove faster than the hard one making a wave pattern with the softer wood lower the harder wood.

Clap Your Wood Tight

The strength of the bond depends on how much clamp pressure is placed to keep the wood together while it's gluing.

Wood Glue and Varnish Don't Bond

You must make sure that there is no varnish on the wood you are gluing. If you use a board that has varnish on it, sand it until the varnish is thoroughly gone.

Save the Sawdust

When you're doing a wood project, save some of the sawdust for each of the woods you used. This makes great putty since it is the exact same color as the wood you're using. All you need to do is mix it with some clear glue and fill in the cracks.
