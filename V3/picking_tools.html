Sanders

Are used to smooth and remove all defects in the wood’s surface. Sandpaper is rate in measurements of grits. The lower the number the rougher the sandpaper. For most wood projects, you will generally want to start with 60 grit sandpaper and work your way up to 150 grit.

Sander come in three type:

  • Square – these are the most common and can be used for most any sanding job.
  • Circular – more resent technology that uses the sandpaper more evenly than the traditional square kind.
  • Belt – These are used for heavy-duty sanding jobs like removing the varnish from a wood floor.

These saws have small blade that oscillates up and down to cut boards up to 1 inch think. They are commonly used to cut intricate patterns out of wood.

Table Saws & Bandsaws

Both saws are used to split boards longwise but here are their differences:

  • Table saw use a circular blade and make a smother cut than a bandsaw.
  • Bandsaws use a belt shaped blade and can make swirly cuts like a scroll saw but on a much larger scale.
